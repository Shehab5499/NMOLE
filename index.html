Our First Project: Shell and Tube Heat Exchanger 2016

Back in 2016, N MOLE's first project was Shell and Tube Heat Exchanger. We managed to design, fabricate, and operate a real industrial equipment for the first time. After suffering a lot, we could find our way out to do something that completely improbable to be achieved by students only. The first step was the most difficult one, however, it was also a very remarkable one. The one that enforced every other step further.
Shell and Tube Heat Exchanger is one of the most common thermal industrial equipment and N MOLE till now has implemented this equipment 3 times (in 2016, 2018, and finally 2019 in the mechanical branch). We successfully could learn and coach how to design, fabricate, and operate a shell-and-tube heat exchanger from A to Z.
The process that was founded in our first project was and still adapted to perform and lead the way to this.
It all begins within N MOLE's staff members who complete all the following steps to qualify themselves to coach this experience and share its knowledge to others. N MOLE coaches formulate a practical problem and struggle their way to its solution. N MOLE coaches do it early with no guidance and you do it now with our guidance and care. But after all we all do the same process to gain similar valuable experience.
N MOLE's Project Recipe:
1. Acquiring and educating the skills to search on necessary technical information
2. Performing and supervising presentation skills to communicate our findings with the rest of the team
3. Brainstorming and cooperating to formulate our problems and possible solutions leading to solving our final case study
4. Procurement and purchasing parts and services needed to fabricate the equipment at the lowest possible cost
5. Fabricate the equipment and perform necessary pre-startup tests
6. Finally, the big day, the operation day where our dream comes true
7. Summarize our entire experience in a technical report (the proposal)
8. Perform a final presentation to communicate our work professionally to everyone.
9. Evaluating all participating teams regarding technicality, teamwork, and their ability to communicate their work.
10. Announcement of the winner team and celebrating the end of a matchless journey



Liquid-Liquid Extraction Packed Column

Our second step, in 2017, was about an interesting equipment that imply the physical phenomenon of liquid-liquid extraction. It is rarely known by any engineering discipline other than chemical engineering and it is rarely visualized in our life yet it exists in numerous industries purifying the most complicated mixture. N MOLE's second project has revealed and conquered another challenge by allowing students to see and interact with liquid-liquid extraction directly and not only through textbooks and pictures.
Case Study
Participating engineering students in each team must perform extensive research about the physical and engineering concepts underlying liquid-liquid extraction and communicate their findings in weekly presentations
Design, fabrication, and operation of a liquid-liquid extraction packed column is due to 3 weeks later starting from the beginning of the project
Each team must figure out how to build the equipment with minimum cost
Maximum degree of separation must be achieved
Organizing tasks and effective communication within in each team is highly considered and evaluated
A final report and presentation about the entire project are submitted and performed by each team to N MOLE's academic committee head

Water Treatment Full Operation

Water treatment are one of the most vital processes in any plant. Together, N MOLE could make a full water treatment operation that purifies dirty water that is highly contaminated to a clean and usable water with dedication and teamwork.

Each team has to be divided into subgroups and present their research every week.
Each team must find their own way to design, fabricate, and operate three equipment (Sand Filter, Carbon Filter, and Ion Exchanger).
Each team must figure out how to construct each equipment with minimum cost

Organizing tasks and effective communication within in each team is highly considered and evaluated

Mechanical Branch: Cooling Tower

Cooling tower is the second project dedicated for mechanical engineering students after Shell-and-tube heat exchanger.
Mechanical branch has founded and produced unique projects that provide real hands-on experience with lots of engineering software involved.
Case Study
A competition was held with two mechanical engineering teams.
Each team should learn the principal of how to search the required engineering knowledge and with minimum time.
With a huge opportunity for every single member to improve and prove their skills, each team undergoes many presentations and brainstorming sessions to come up with the most effective solution a cooling tower.
Teams apply various engineering concepts using MATLAB to solve the design problem. SolidWorks to design and visualize and COMOL Mutliphysics to undergo a delicate simulation for the final design.
Finally, Each team must find their own way out to design, fabricate, and operate the cooling tower and with minimum cost.
Organizing tasks and effective communication within in each team is highly considered and evaluated
A final report and presentation about the entire project are submitted and performed by each team to N MOLE's academic committee head

Liquid-Liquid Extraction Sieve Plate Column - National Competition

One of our greatest projects ever! Liquid-Liquid Extraction 2 (LLE 2), the project was like a part two to our previous project LLE packed column. The project was a huge national competition between chemical engineering students from Alexandria, Cairo, and Minia Universities.

MECA Branch: Air-Cooled Heat Exchanger

With another exciting and famous thermal equipment, our project "Air-Cooled Heat Exchanger" was finally launched in 2021 for mechanical engineering students. The implementation of this project was a masterpiece beginning from the high spirit of each of the two participating teams till the end of the project with the well-organized and spectacular presentations.
The project was special again with the extensive use of engineering software like SolidWorks, COMSOL Mutliphysics, and MATLAB. Each was required to find various design aspects including thermal, mechanical, and pressure drop design considerations. With every concept digested by a team's subgroup, it was presented to rest of the team in a time-efficient manner.
Case Study
The design, fabrication, and operation of an Air-Cooled HX was due with the following success criteria:
Cooling 50 liters of hot water from 75 celisuis to room temperature within only 4 mins of operation
Allowable budget must not exceed 2000 L.E.
The quality of implementation and team collaboration is the most important aspect for each team's score
A final report and presentation about the entire project are submitted and performed by each team to N MOLE's academic committee head

Ammonia Gas Scrubber

In 2021, we have launched an entire new project and another real challenge which was dealing with a liquid-gas system. with low budget, high performance and ensuring all safety considerations.
This competition in this project was to design, fabricate, and operate a packed-bed scrubber column in order to purify gas mixture of air & ammonia gas.
Case Study
The design, fabrication, and operation of an Ammonia gas scrubber packed column was due with the following success criteria:
Purifying exhaust gas with 10% ammonia by volume by calculated efficiency of 80%.
Packing material of plastic raschig ring (15 mm) should be used
Exhaust gas flowrate of 2.3 m3/min
All team members should acquire the technicality for the project and communicate effectively to complete and report their work
A final report and presentation about the entire project are submitted and performed by each team to N MOLE's academic committee head
